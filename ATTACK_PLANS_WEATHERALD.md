## The plan, restated from the data

England's operating pattern to Weatherald, on this evidence:

1. **Live at a good length and just short of it, around off** — and *never* drop genuinely short.
   The 10 m+ ball, a sixth of the diet to Head/Carey/Khawaja, effectively vanished for him.
2. **Give width only when full** — the short-wide ball he cuts (and the shorter one he pulls) was
   rationed to ~26 balls across five Tests. When one did arrive he scored at SR ~110 off it.
3. **The wicket ball is full at the stumps, against his leg-side scoring shots.** All four LBWs
   came with him playing *Worked* or *Flick* — his across-the-front-pad scoring strokes — coded
   *Hit pad*. The stump attack converts his own leg-side game into the dismissal.

The one correction to the spoken version of the claim: "short" was the *setup zone he was held in*
(back of a length), not a bouncer plan — the data shows the opposite of a short-ball assault.
